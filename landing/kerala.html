Spices of Kerala

By Vikram Singh - July 5, 2025

Kerala, often called the "Spice Garden of India," is a paradise for food lovers with its rich tapestry of aromatic spices. This lush state, nestled along the Malabar Coast, has been a global trading hub for spices like black pepper, cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves for centuries.

The journey through Kerala's spice plantations is a sensory delight. Visiting the verdant spice gardens in Thekkady or Wayanad, you can see how cardamom pods are hand-picked, and black pepper vines climb tall trees. These spices are the heart of Kerala's cuisine, adding depth to dishes like **Malabar Fish Curry** and **Appam with Stew**. The annual **Spice Festival** in Idukki celebrates this legacy with cooking demonstrations, spice markets, and cultural performances.

Beyond the kitchen, spices play a role in Ayurvedic practices, with turmeric and ginger used for their medicinal properties. A trip to Kerala is incomplete without tasting the authentic flavors and learning the art of spice blending from local experts.

Tips for Exploring Kerala's Spices

  • Visit spice plantations during the harvest season (October to March).
  • Participate in a cooking class to learn spice usage.
  • Purchase fresh spices from local markets for authenticity.
  • Try Ayurvedic treatments using local spices.

About the Author

Vikram Singh is a food and travel writer with a passion for exploring India's culinary diversity. His journeys through Kerala have deepened his appreciation for its spices and traditional recipes.
